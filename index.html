New to SIP? Start here

Everything you need to know, in plain language.

📌 What is SIP?

SIP stands for Systematic Investment Plan. It means investing a fixed amount in a mutual fund every month — like a recurring deposit, but in the stock market. Even ₹100/month works!

💡 Why SIP beats lump sum

With SIP, you don't need to time the market. You buy more units when prices are low and fewer when prices are high — this is called rupee cost averaging. Over time, it smooths out market ups and downs.

📈 How SIP builds wealth over time

₹500/month for 10 years at 12% return = ₹1.16 lakhs invested → ₹2.3 lakhs in value. The magic is compounding — your returns earn returns too!

💡 The earlier you start, the more powerful compounding becomes. Starting at 20 vs 30 can double your final amount.

🚫 Common beginner mistakes

Avoid these to protect your investments:

  • Stopping SIP when market falls (that's actually the best time!)
  • Investing more than you can afford
  • Expecting quick profits — SIP is a 5+ year game
  • Not reviewing your SIP amount yearly
